COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Commercial Lease Agreement ("Lease") is entered into as of June 1, 2024, between Metro Properties LLC ("Landlord") and BrightIdeas Co. ("Tenant").

ARTICLE 1 - PREMISES

1.1 Leased Premises. Landlord leases to Tenant, and Tenant leases from Landlord, the commercial space located at 500 Innovation Drive, Suite 300, San Jose, California 95134 (the "Premises"), comprising approximately 2,500 square feet.

1.2 Common Areas. Tenant shall have the right to use common areas including hallways, restrooms, elevators, and parking facilities in accordance with rules established by Landlord.

ARTICLE 2 - TERM

2.1 Initial Term. The initial term of this Lease shall be three (3) years, commencing on July 1, 2024 and ending on June 30, 2027 ("Initial Term").

2.2 Renewal Option. Tenant shall have the option to renew this Lease for two (2) additional one-year terms, provided Tenant gives written notice of intent to renew at least ninety (90) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

2.3 Holdover. If Tenant remains in possession after expiration without a renewal agreement, the tenancy shall convert to month-to-month at one hundred fifty percent (150%) of the last monthly rent.

ARTICLE 3 - RENT

3.1 Base Rent. Tenant shall pay monthly base rent of $6,250 (2,500 sq ft × $2.50/sq ft), payable in advance on the first day of each month.

3.2 Security Deposit. Tenant has deposited $12,500 (two months' rent) as security, to be held by Landlord and applied against damages or unpaid rent upon termination.

3.3 Rent Escalation. Base rent shall increase by three percent (3%) annually on each anniversary of the commencement date.

3.4 Late Fees. Rent not paid within five (5) days of the due date shall incur a late fee of five percent (5%) of the monthly rent, plus interest at one percent (1%) per month.

3.5 Additional Rent. In addition to base rent, Tenant shall pay:
(a) Pro rata share of property taxes (estimated at $500/month)
(b) Pro rata share of insurance (estimated at $200/month)
(c) Pro rata share of common area maintenance (estimated at $300/month)

ARTICLE 4 - USE OF PREMISES

4.1 Permitted Use. The Premises shall be used solely for general office purposes and software development activities.

4.2 Prohibited Uses. Tenant shall not use the Premises for:
(a) Any illegal activity
(b) Manufacturing or heavy industrial operations
(c) Any use that creates excessive noise, odors, or vibrations
(d) Any use that increases insurance premiums

4.3 Compliance with Laws. Tenant shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws, regulations, and ordinances.

ARTICLE 5 - MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

5.1 Landlord's Obligations. Landlord shall maintain:
(a) Structural elements including roof, exterior walls, and foundation
(b) Common areas
(c) Building systems including HVAC, plumbing, and electrical (excluding tenant improvements)

5.2 Tenant's Obligations. Tenant shall maintain:
(a) Interior non-structural repairs
(b) Lighting and light fixtures within the Premises
(c) Regular cleaning and janitorial services
(d) Any damage caused by Tenant's negligence or misuse

ARTICLE 6 - ALTERATIONS AND IMPROVEMENTS

6.1 Landlord's Consent. Tenant shall not make any alterations or improvements without Landlord's prior written consent, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld for non-structural changes.

6.2 Ownership. All improvements shall become the property of Landlord upon installation, except for Tenant's trade fixtures and equipment.

6.3 Restoration. Upon termination, Tenant shall remove its trade fixtures and restore the Premises to its original condition, reasonable wear and tear excepted.

ARTICLE 7 - INSURANCE AND INDEMNITY

7.1 Tenant's Insurance. Tenant shall maintain:
(a) Commercial general liability insurance with limits of at least $2,000,000 per occurrence
(b) Property insurance covering Tenant's personal property and improvements
(c) Workers' compensation insurance as required by law

7.2 Indemnification. Tenant shall indemnify and hold harmless Landlord from all claims, damages, and expenses arising from Tenant's use of the Premises, except to the extent caused by Landlord's negligence or willful misconduct.

ARTICLE 8 - DEFAULT AND REMEDIES

8.1 Events of Default. The following shall constitute events of default:
(a) Failure to pay rent within ten (10) days after written notice
(b) Breach of any other provision that continues for thirty (30) days after written notice
(c) Bankruptcy, insolvency, or assignment for benefit of creditors
(d) Abandonment of the Premises

8.2 Landlord's Remedies. Upon default, Landlord may:
(a) Terminate the Lease and retake possession
(b) Accelerate all remaining rent
(c) Pursue any legal or equitable remedies
(d) Apply the security deposit to unpaid rent and damages

ARTICLE 9 - GENERAL PROVISIONS

9.1 Assignment and Subletting. Tenant shall not assign this Lease or sublet the Premises without Landlord's prior written consent, not to be unreasonably withheld.

9.2 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered by certified mail to the addresses set forth above.

9.3 Governing Law. This Lease shall be governed by the laws of the State of California.

9.4 Entire Agreement. This Lease constitutes the entire agreement and supersedes all prior negotiations and agreements.

9.5 Severability. If any provision is held invalid, the remainder of the Lease shall continue in full force and effect.
